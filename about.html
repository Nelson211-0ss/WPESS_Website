Our Journey

Our Story & Mission

Our Beginning

Women Peace Envoy South Sudan (WPESS) organization started as a humble community group of women in 2019 in response to a crisis caused by the internecine civil wars that caused internal displacement, poverty and poor health indices especially among women and children in rural communities of Magwi, Eastern Equatoria, South Sudan.

Our Growth

Today, Women Peace Envoy South Sudan (WPESS) has grown to cover over 6 Payams of Magwi County and growing wider to other Counties and soon she will be to other States of South Sudan. Women Peace Envoy South Sudan (WPESS) cherishes the respect and admiration that the organization has gained from the local communities of Magwi and from international organizations i.e. UNCHR and GIZ.

Our Mission

(WPESS) exists to promote better life for most vulnerable women, girls and Children (Children) in Magwi and South Sudan through the provision of services in critical and priority sectors for the local communities within which the organization works.

Our Programs

WPESS development programs focus mainly on GBV and General Protection, Peace Building, Human Rights Activities (including mitigation of gender-based violence [GBV], Advocacy and Child Protection), Water, Sanitation and Hygiene (WASH) interventions, Food security and Livelihood, Education, and Institutional capacity building.

Our Impact

These initiatives provided Magwi WPESS with a considerable acquired experience and competency in project development and in particular in the context of community-based integrated programs in the rural and urban areas of South Sudan. Women Peace Envoy South Sudan has been particularly successful in developing mass-education awareness and campaigns about SGBV, dangers associated with HIV/AIDS. Girl child education, food security issues, water and hygiene campaigns through widespread dissemination of information, using a wide range of information, education and communication (IEC) materials and approaches.

In addition, specifically targeted groups at-risk of HIV/AIDS received focused education through peer-educator models. Furthermore, WPESS has carried out awareness campaigns for the victims of war support and formed victim support groups in the community of Magwi County) and followed up the clients with an integrated care model than not only includes referrals and linkages to care and treatment but also income generating activities. Another important strategy has been the capacity building of other community-based organizations (CBOs).

Rose Anyek John

Founder & Executive Director

A visionary leader and advocate for women's rights, Rose Anyek John founded WPESS in 2020 with a mission to empower women and promote peace in South Sudan. Her dedication to women's empowerment and community development has made her a respected figure in the region.

With over 20 years of experience in community development and women's empowerment, she has been instrumental in shaping the organization's vision and impact. Her leadership has guided WPESS in implementing successful programs in peacebuilding, gender-based violence prevention, and women's economic empowerment.

Under her direction, WPESS has established partnerships with international organizations including UNHCR and GIZ, expanding its reach to multiple communities across Magwi County. She has been a driving force behind initiatives that have improved access to education, healthcare, and economic opportunities for women and children in South Sudan.

Rose's commitment to sustainable development and community engagement has earned her recognition as a leading advocate for women's rights and peacebuilding in South Sudan. Her work continues to inspire and empower women across the region to become agents of change in their communities.
